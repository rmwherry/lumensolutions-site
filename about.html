Philosophy

The instinct came first.

The data instinct started in 1994 — a kid in Anamosa, Iowa noticing that the numbers in a baseball simulation game told a story, if you knew how to read them. That impulse became the through-line for everything that followed: find the system, understand it underneath, make it visible and actionable.

Over 21 years at Macy's and Paycor, the tools changed. The instinct didn't. 200+ Power BI dashboards, a 12-person analyst team, metrics that actually moved the business. A 15% reduction in client loss revenue. First-contact resolution from 75% to 90%+ sustained. These weren't reporting projects — they were system interventions.

Lumen Solutions LLC is the same instinct applied directly: no org chart in the way, no layers between the problem and the work. Just a clear problem, a capable partner, and the minimum viable toolkit for the room you're about to walk into.

How the Work Works

Every engagement runs the same four moves.

Clients arrive in one of two states: scared with analysis paralysis, or confident with analysis paralysis. Both leave with the same thing: actionable clarity and language to navigate the next room without sounding coached.

01 — Triage

Find the real problem.

Name the problem underneath the one they walked in with. Most clients know something is wrong. Fewer know what. Triage is about getting to the actual constraint before touching anything else.

02 — Mapping

Connect it to what’s known.

Connect the client’s situation to relevant standards, analogous cases, and counterparty posture. Compress the research into the minimum viable frame for the decision they’re about to make.

03 — Vocabulary

Give them the language.

Transfer the vocabulary they need to navigate the next conversation — with a vendor, a board, a donor, a regulator — without sounding scripted. Fluency, not talking points.

04 — Decision Architecture

Structure what they’ll actually face.

Build around the real decision tree: two branching paths, honest tradeoffs, clear exits. Not an idealized scenario. What actually happens if they go left, and what happens if they go right.
